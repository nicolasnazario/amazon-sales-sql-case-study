# Mercedes-Benz Sales SQL Analysis 

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a lareg scale Mercedes-Benz sales datset (1.8 million rows) using SQL to uncover key business insights related to pricing strategy, product performance, and worldwide demand. The objective of this project is to simulate a real-world business analysis scenario by identifying revenue drivers, understanding customer demand patterns, and evakuating the imnpact of vehicle characteristics such as horsepower, fuel type, and turbo features. 

---

## Tools Used

- PostgreSQL
- pgAdmin
- SQL (Aggregations, CASE Statements, Window Functions) 

---

## Dataset Description 

The dataset contains 1.8 million Mercedes_Benz sales with the following fields:

- Model
- Year
- Region
- Color
- Fuel Type
- Base Price
- Horsepower
- Sales Volume
- Turbo (True/False) 

---

## Business Questions Analyzed

1. Which models generate the highest revenue?
2. How does pricing impact sales volume and revenue?
3. Do turbocharged vehicles perform better than non-turbo vehicles 
4. How does horsepower relate to vehicle pricing?
5. What are the key trends in sales over time?

---

## Key Insights 

- Entry-level vehicles (A-class, CLA, GLA) contribute significantly tot total sales volume due to affordability
- Mid-range vehicles, particularly the GLC, rank as the top selling models, indicating strong demand in the mid-tier segment
- Luxury models such as the S-class show lower sales volume, highlighting the impact of higher pricing on demand
- The data demonstrates an inverse relagtionship between price and sales volume, with mid-range vehicles balncing demand and profitabilty 
- Turbocharged vehicles show much higher sales than non-turbo vehicles
- Customers prefer petrol vehciles over other vehicle types by a large margin
