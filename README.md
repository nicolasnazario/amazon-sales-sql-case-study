# Amazon Sales SQL Case Study

## Project Overview

This project analyzes 50,000 Amazon sales transactions using PostgreSQL to identify 
revenue drivers, product performance trends, discount and rating effectiveness, and regional contribution patterns.

The objective was to simulate a real-world business analysis scenario and extract actionable insights using SQL.

---

## Tools Used

- PostgreSQL
- pgAdmin
- SQL (CTEs, Aggregations, Window Functions, Date Functions)

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains 50,000 Amazon sales transactions including:

- Order ID
- Product ID
- Product Category
- Quantity Sold
- Total Revenue
- Customer Rating
- Discount Percentage
- Customer Region
- Order Date
- Price
- Payment Method 
- Rating 
- Review count 
- Discounted Price

---

## Business Questions Analyzed

1. What are the overall revenue and order KPIs?
2. Which product categories drive the most revenue?
3. What percentage of total revenue does each category contribute?
4. How does revenue vary by region?
5. Do higher ratings correlate with higher revenue?
6. Do higher discounts increase order volume or reduce revenue?
7. What are the top 10 revenue-generating products?
8. What are the monthly revenue trends?
9. What products are purchased the most?

---

## Key Insights

- Total revenue exceeded $32M across 50,000 transactions.
- Revenue is concentrated in a small number of product categories.
- Regional performance varies significantly, indicating geographic opportunity.
- Higher discounts increase unit volume but do not proportionally increase average revenue per order.
- Customer ratings show moderate correlation with revenue performance.
- Revenue trends indicate potential seasonality patterns.
